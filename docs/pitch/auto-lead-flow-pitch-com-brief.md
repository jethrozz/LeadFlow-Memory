# Auto Lead Flow Pitch.com Deck Brief

Use this document as a copy-ready brief for generating an English pitch deck in Pitch.com.

## One-Line Direction

Create a polished 9-slide startup demo deck for **Auto Lead Flow**, a verifiable social-platform lead automation system that discovers high-intent leads, qualifies them, reaches out through a real Android phone running Xiaohongshu, and continues personalized follow-up with long-term memory and auditable Walrus records.

## Project Name

**Auto Lead Flow**

Optional product description:

**Verifiable Social Lead Automation for Configurable Sales Agents**

## Audience

Hackathon judges, Web3 infrastructure reviewers, and AI agent builders. The deck should quickly communicate the product value, demo flow, and Walrus relevance without feeling like a technical architecture document.

## Core Message

Auto Lead Flow turns buying intent expressed on social platforms into real, personalized, verifiable outreach. It helps configurable sales agents discover leads from posts and comments, extract structured customer profiles, send first-touch messages through a real mobile app account, remember customer context across sessions, and store decision evidence as verifiable artifacts.

## What To Emphasize

- Social-platform lead discovery: find buying intent from posts and comments before the lead enters a CRM.
- Real outreach: send personalized messages through a real Android phone running the Xiaohongshu app.
- Configurable agent roles: industry, role, tone, qualification fields, and follow-up strategy are driven by playbooks.
- Long-term customer memory: customer facts and prior context persist across sessions and workers.
- Verifiable automation: Walrus stores source evidence, scoring reports, outreach decisions, conversation traces, and handoff proofs.
- The current demo is real-estate sales on Xiaohongshu, but the engine can adapt to other high-consideration social lead-generation scenarios.

## What To Avoid

- Do not make the deck look like a generic CRM pitch.
- Do not over-focus on implementation details or code.
- Do not use Chinese text in the slides.
- Do not invent metrics, traction, revenue, user counts, or market size numbers.
- Do not present the project as only a memory system; the key product is end-to-end social lead automation.

## Visual Style

Create a clean, formal, modern B2B SaaS / AI infrastructure deck.

Suggested style:

- Format: 16:9 widescreen.
- Palette: deep teal / near-black for authority, cyan for automation and signal, coral for outreach / conversion moments, light gray background for clarity.
- Typography: modern sans-serif, strong bold titles, readable body text.
- Layout: spacious, structured, diagram-driven.
- Use flow diagrams, evidence cards, mobile outreach motifs, signal/radar motifs, and clean agent-to-customer visual metaphors.
- Avoid cartoon robots, playful mascots, excessive gradients, stock-photo-heavy layouts, or decorative blobs.
- Use the uploaded logo if available. Local reference asset: `assets/brand/logo.jpg`.

## Suggested Slide Structure

### Slide 1: Cover

Title:

**Auto Lead Flow**

Subtitle:

**Verifiable Social Lead Automation for Configurable Sales Agents**

Supporting line:

Automated social lead discovery, real outreach, and persistent follow-up.

Visual:

Use the Auto Lead Flow logo. Add a compact flow strip:

Discover -> Qualify -> Outreach -> Follow-up

### Slide 2: Problem

Title:

**Social Lead Generation Is Still Too Manual**

Subtitle:

Social acquisition still depends on manual search, judgment, and repeated outreach.

Three problem cards:

**Manual discovery**

Sales teams scroll posts and comments to find people who may be ready to buy.

**Scattered context**

Budget, location, urgency, concerns, and past replies are split across conversations.

**Hard to audit**

It is difficult to prove why a lead was scored or why a message was sent.

Bottom takeaway:

**High-frequency work, low automation, fragile memory.**

### Slide 3: Opportunity

Title:

**High-Intent Leads Already Express Needs Online**

Subtitle:

Many potential customers already reveal clear buying needs in social content.

Example social post/comment:

> "Looking for a three-bedroom near the High-Tech District. Budget matters, and commute should be easy."

Show extracted fields:

- Budget: price-sensitive
- Location: high-tech district
- Layout: three-bedroom
- Concern: commute
- Intent: active buyer

Takeaway:

This is not a cold lead. It is a visible buying signal.

### Slide 4: Solution Flow

Title:

**From Social Intent to Real Outreach**

Subtitle:

A configurable sales-agent workflow for discovery, qualification, real outreach, and follow-up.

Flow:

Social Listening -> Qualify Lead -> Memory Recall -> Real-Device Outreach -> Reply Polling -> Follow-up Loop

Main statement:

**The agent role, industry scenario, tone, qualification fields, and follow-up strategy are configurable through playbooks.**

Supporting line:

Playbooks make the same engine adaptable across real estate and other social lead-generation scenarios.

### Slide 5: Demo Flow

Title:

**Demo: From Xiaohongshu Signal to Real Outreach**

Subtitle:

The demo shows the complete loop from social intent to real DM outreach and continued follow-up.

Six steps:

1. **Discover**  
   Find buyer intent in posts/comments.

2. **Extract**  
   Capture budget, location, layout, and concerns.

3. **Score**  
   Decide whether the lead is worth outreach.

4. **Reach out**  
   Send a personalized DM through a real Android app.

5. **Listen**  
   Poll customer replies and sync transcript.

6. **Continue**  
   Recall context and generate the next follow-up.

Bottom takeaway:

**Dashboard shows lead status, timeline, memory, outreach records, and verifiable artifacts.**

### Slide 6: Core Capabilities

Title:

**Core Capabilities**

Subtitle:

Built around the real social acquisition workflow, not just a CRM screen.

Four capability cards:

**Social Lead Discovery**

Search posts and comments for buying intent, then turn unstructured social content into leads.

**Real-Device Outreach**

Send first-touch private messages through a real social app account on a real Android phone.

**Configurable Industry Agent**

Use playbooks to configure role, tone, qualification fields, and follow-up strategy.

**Long-Term Follow-up**

Recall customer context across sessions and workers, then continue multi-touch conversion.

### Slide 7: Why Walrus

Title:

**Why Walrus: Make Agent Actions Verifiable**

Subtitle:

Agent automation should not be a black box; every business action can leave a verifiable record.

Main diagram:

Black-box automation -> Verifiable business records

Proof artifact labels:

- Source evidence
- Scoring report
- Outreach decision
- Conversation trace
- Handoff proof

Supporting line:

Walrus stores immutable artifacts that explain what the agent saw, decided, sent, and handed off.

### Slide 8: Beyond Real Estate

Title:

**Beyond Real Estate**

Subtitle:

The demo starts with Xiaohongshu and real-estate sales, but the system is a configurable industry lead agent.

Center object:

**Playbook-Configured Sales Agent**

Scenario chips:

- Real Estate
- Automotive
- Education
- Insurance
- Home Renovation
- B2B SaaS

Bottom takeaway:

Any high-consideration sales workflow with social intent, repeated outreach, and long decision cycles can adapt the same engine.

### Slide 9: Closing

Title:

**Turn Social Intent into Verifiable Outreach**

Subtitle:

Discover, qualify, reach out, and follow up with high-intent social leads automatically.

Supporting line:

Social buying intent becomes verifiable, persistent customer outreach.

End flow:

Discover -> Qualify -> Outreach -> Follow-up

5. Long-term memory keeps customer context alive across sessions and workers.
6. Walrus makes the automation accountable by storing evidence and decision artifacts.
